An FPV (First-Person View) Drone is a type of unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) that offers an immersive, "cockpit-view" flying experience,
 designed for high speed, agility, and manual control. Unlike traditional camera drones (like the DJI Mavic series), which are designed
  for autonomous stability and stabilization, FPV drones require the pilot to wear specialized goggles that stream a live, low-latency 
  video feed directly from a camera mounted on the drone’s nose. This perspective makes the pilot feel as though they are sitting inside
   the aircraft, allowing them to fly through tight spaces, perform complex aerobatic maneuvers, and capture dynamic footage that 
   standard drones cannot
